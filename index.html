About Us

À Propos de Nous

Petit Ilot is founded by Lucas Couderc and Nadège Carra, two passionate professionals dedicated to children's development through play.


Lucas Couderc - Owner/Manager: Graduate in Sports Science with 10 years of experience in children's sports animation, bringing expertise in physical development and motor skills.


Nadège Carra - Co-Manager: Kindergarten teacher for 5 years with specialized experience working with children with specific needs, ensuring inclusive and adapted programming.


Together, we're creating Montreal's first indoor play space combining motor skills, symbolic play, and sensory experiences in a safe, high-quality environment designed for free play and family bonding.

Petit Ilot est fondé par Lucas Couderc et Nadège Carra, deux professionnels passionnés dédiés au développement des enfants par le jeu.


Lucas Couderc - Propriétaire/Gérant : Diplômé en sciences du sport avec 10 ans d'expérience dans l'animation sportive pour enfants, apportant une expertise en développement physique et motricité.


Nadège Carra - Co-gérante : Enseignante en maternelle depuis 5 ans avec une expérience spécialisée auprès d'enfants à besoins spécifiques, assurant une programmation inclusive et adaptée.


Ensemble, nous créons le premier espace de jeux intérieur de Montréal combinant motricité, jeux symboliques et expériences sensorielles dans un environnement sécuritaire et de haute qualité conçu pour le jeu libre et les liens familiaux.

Why Choose Petit Ilot?

Pourquoi Choisir Petit Ilot?

✓ All equipment meets Canadian (CSA Z614) and European (EN 1176) safety standards
✓ Limited capacity (30 children per session) for a premium, safe experience
✓ No screens, no food service - focus on play and family bonding
✓ Equipment designed to support adult weight for active parent participation
✓ Age-specific time slots ensure appropriate activities and safety

✓ Pas d'écrans, pas de restauration - focus sur le jeu et les liens familiaux
✓ Équipements conçus pour supporter le poids des adultes pour une participation parentale active
✓ Créneaux par tranche d'âge assurent activités appropriées et sécurité

Project Launch Announcement

Annonce du Lancement du Projet

Today marks an exciting milestone in our journey - the official launch of Petit Ilot! After months of planning, research, and preparation, we're thrilled to share our vision for Montreal's most innovative indoor play space designed specifically for children aged 0-10 years and their families.

Our Mission

At Petit Ilot, we believe that play is the foundation of childhood development. Our mission is to create a safe, engaging environment where children can explore, learn, and grow through child-initiated free play while fostering meaningful connections between parents and their little ones.

What Makes Us Different

Unlike traditional play centers, Petit Ilot focuses on:

  • Three distinct developmental zones designed by child development experts
  • Equipment that supports active parent participation
  • Screen-free environment promoting real-world interaction
  • Limited capacity ensuring a premium, safe experience
  • Age-appropriate time slots for optimal development

The Journey Ahead

Over the coming months, we'll be sharing our progress as we search for the perfect location, select our equipment, and prepare for our grand opening. We're committed to transparency and want our future families to be part of this exciting journey from the very beginning.

Finding the Perfect Location

Trouver l'Emplacement Parfait

The search for our ideal location is well underway, and we're excited to share what we're looking for and why location is so crucial to the Petit Ilot experience.

Our Location Criteria

We're seeking a special space that meets our high standards:

  • Size: Minimum 3,500 square feet to accommodate our three distinct play zones
  • Ceiling Height: At least 12 feet to safely install our climbing structures and play equipment
  • Accessibility: Ground floor with excellent wheelchair and stroller access
  • Parking: Ample parking for families with young children
  • Safety: Secure location with controlled access and visibility

Target Neighborhoods

We're focusing our search on family-friendly neighborhoods, particularly Villeray and surrounding areas. These communities are known for their strong family presence, walkability, and access to public transportation - all important factors for our families.

Current Progress

We've already visited several promising locations and are working closely with commercial real estate agents who understand our unique requirements. Each potential space is being evaluated not just for its physical attributes, but for how well it will serve our community of families.

Stay tuned for updates as we get closer to announcing our future home!

Equipment Selection & Safety Standards

Sélection d'Équipements & Normes de Sécurité

Safety is our top priority at Petit Ilot, and we're taking no shortcuts when it comes to selecting equipment that will provide years of safe, developmental play for Montreal families.

Safety Standards We Follow

All our equipment will meet or exceed:

  • Canadian Standards (CSA Z614): Playground equipment and surfacing requirements
  • European Standards (EN 1176): Additional safety requirements for playground equipment
  • Health Canada Guidelines: For child-safe materials and finishes
  • Fire Safety Codes: Meeting all municipal requirements

Equipment Selection Process

We're working exclusively with certified suppliers who specialize in commercial-grade play equipment. Each piece is being selected not just for fun, but for its developmental benefits and ability to support children of varying abilities and needs.

Special Features

What sets our equipment apart:

  • Adult-Weight Capacity: All equipment designed to support parent participation
  • Sensory Integration: Textures, sounds, and visual elements that support sensory development
  • Adaptive Design: Equipment accessible to children with varying physical abilities
  • Easy Maintenance: Antimicrobial surfaces and easy-to-clean materials
